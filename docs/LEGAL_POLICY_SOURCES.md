# Legal Policy Source Notes

> Last reviewed: 20 July 2026. This file records the official sources used to
> keep public policy copy factual. It is not a substitute for review by qualified
> Spanish legal counsel before full commercial launch.

## Data protection

- AEPD, **Right to information**: the detailed privacy layer should identify the
  controller, purposes, legal basis, retention criteria, recipients, rights,
  withdrawal of consent and the right to complain.
  <https://www.aepd.es/derechos-y-deberes/conoce-tus-derechos/derecho-de-informacion>
- AEPD, **Exercise your rights**: access, rectification, objection, deletion,
  restriction, portability and protection from solely automated individual decisions.
  <https://www.aepd.es/derechos-y-deberes/ejerce-tus-derechos>
- AEPD complaint guidance: a person may complain to the AEPD after first exercising
  the relevant right with the controller.
  <https://www.aepd.es/preguntas-frecuentes/13-reclamaciones/FAQ-1303-puedo-interponer-una-reclamacion-respecto-al-ejercicio-de-los-derechos-de-proteccion-de-datos>

## Records and invoices

- Royal Decree 1619/2012, Article 19: invoices and related documents must be retained
  for the period provided by Spanish tax law.
  <https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2012-14696>
- Spanish Commercial Code, Article 30: business books, correspondence, documentation
  and supporting records are generally retained for six years, subject to special rules.
  <https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-1885-6627>

## Operational policy boundaries

- Cancellation percentages, fulfilment choices, deposits and amendment rules must
  always match the implemented booking flow and the visible refund policy.
- The current online checkout does not automatically add a security deposit.
- Optional Google Analytics is consent-gated; the choice is stored in browser local
  storage under `rentanything_analytics_consent`.
- Public policy wording deliberately uses retention criteria rather than claiming one
  universal period for every category of personal data.
