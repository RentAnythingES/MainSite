# RentAnything.es — Product Sense
> Last updated: 2026-07-08

## Mission
Become **the first destination** when anyone searches for equipment rental in Spain. Starting with Valencia, scaling Spain-wide.

## Business Model

### Three Supply Channels
1. **Direct inventory** — Equipment we own and rent out (highest margin, full quality control)
2. **Marketplace** — Third-party providers list on our platform (commission-based, scaled inventory)
3. **Arbitrage** — We rent from other sources and re-rent with margin (gap-filling, demand validation)

### Revenue Streams
- Rental fees (per-day tiered pricing)
- Delivery/pickup fees
- Marketplace commission (from third-party providers)
- Potential: Babonbo listing (20% cut — use as distribution channel, always prefer direct queries)

### Platform Positioning
Babonbo, BabyQuip, Cloud of Goods are NOT pure competitors — they're platforms where we can list. But we always prefer direct bookings (0% commission vs 20%). Our SEO strategy should make direct discovery the primary channel.

RentAnything.es should not become a generic rental catalogue. The brand promise is:

> **Travel light. Feel at home.**

The site should help visitors make a Valencia stay easier, more comfortable, and less stressful. The product grid matters, but the main customer story is the stay: arriving with a baby, spending a week at the beach, working from a temporary apartment, travelling with grandparents, or needing local accessibility support.

### Expansion Path
Valencia → Costa Blanca (Alicante, Benidorm) → Barcelona → Málaga → Madrid → Spain-wide

## Target Audiences
1. **Families with babies** — Travel cots, strollers, high chairs, baby baths, monitors, carriers
2. **Families with kids** — Beach kits, scooters, balance bikes, toy boxes, outdoor/activity gear
3. **Elderly visitors / mobility needs** — Wheelchairs, scooters, walkers, shower chairs, comfort items
4. **Digital nomads / remote workers** — Monitors, standing desks, ergonomic chairs, keyboards, hubs
5. **Long-stay visitors** — Portable AC, fans, kitchen upgrades, apartment comfort items
6. **Hosts / property managers** — Guest-ready kits without buying, storing, cleaning, or maintaining every item

## Category Architecture

Categories should be customer-intuitive and expandable. We should separate baby needs from older-kid/family needs rather than treating all family travel as one bucket.

Priority category direction:

1. **Baby & Toddler** — Sleep, feeding, stroller, hygiene, and arrival setup
2. **Kids & Family** — Scooters, beach toys, activity gear, toy packs, family mobility
3. **Beach & Outdoor** — Shade, coolers, beach wagons, chairs, towels, picnic gear
4. **Remote Work** — Screens, ergonomic setup, desks, accessories
5. **Apartment Comfort** — Cooling, air quality, kitchen, bedding, long-stay upgrades
6. **Mobility & Accessibility** — Wheelchairs, rollators, scooters, bathroom safety, senior travel support
7. **Sports & Wellness** — Tennis, padel, training equipment, selected fitness and recovery items
8. **Pregnancy & Postpartum** — Comfort, nursing, recovery, carefully scoped hygiene-sensitive items
9. **Host Services** — Recurring kits for Airbnb hosts, aparthotels, relocation agencies, and property managers

Future categories should be added only when they have either real demand, strong SEO logic, clear partner supply, or a bundle use case.

## Bundles / Kits Strategy

Bundles are the intended commercial layer between useful Valencia guides and individual product pages. Individual items capture bottom-funnel search demand; bundles reduce decision load and raise order value.

Initial bundle surfaces to build:

1. **Family Beach Kit** — Beach wagon/trolley, shade, cooler, chairs, towels, toys, waterproof pouch
2. **Baby Arrival Kit** — Cot, high chair, stroller, baby bath, play mat, monitor, blackout add-ons
3. **Toddler City Kit** — Compact stroller, scooter or balance bike, helmet, snack tray, toy pack
4. **Remote Work Apartment Kit** — Monitor, chair, laptop stand, keyboard, mouse, USB-C hub, optional desk
5. **Summer Apartment Survival Kit** — Portable AC, fan, blackout blind, air purifier, cooler, beach shade
6. **Accessible Valencia Kit** — Wheelchair or rollator, shower chair, raised toilet seat, optional scooter
7. **Grandparents Visiting Kit** — Mobility support plus comfort and heat-management items
8. **Long-Stay Kitchen Upgrade Kit** — Air fryer/multicooker, blender, coffee machine, child dinnerware

The future booking system should support configurable bundles: fixed starter kits, optional add-ons, substitutions, availability-aware item selection, and staff-reviewed custom requests when inventory is not yet owned.

## Brand Partnership Surfaces

Brand partnerships matter later because they can create premium demo fleets, partner pricing, affiliate revenue, and case studies for larger brands.

The site should eventually include smart, non-spammy surfaces for partner visibility:

- Co-branded kit pages for narrow pilots, such as a family beach kit or remote-work setup
- Brand/product badges inside relevant kits, not generic ad placements
- Post-rental purchase links or discount codes where the user has actually tried the product
- Usage-reporting metrics: rental days, bundle attachment, customer feedback, nationality/language, clicks
- Case-study pages after proof exists, aimed at future brand/distributor outreach

Partnership claims must stay factual. Do not imply endorsement, exclusivity, review volume, or customer proof before it exists.

## Core Value Props (rank order)
1. **Convenience** — Delivered to your door, same-day available
2. **Trust** — Clean, well-maintained, premium brands
3. **Savings** — Rent vs buy, especially for short stays
4. **Local expertise** — We know Valencia, we'll help you plan

## Brand Voice
**Friendly + Trustworthy.** We talk to families, elderly, and nomads — people who want a caring, helpful service that goes the extra mile. Not corporate. Not salesy. Like a knowledgeable friend who lives in Valencia.

## Language Strategy

Spain receives **97 million international visitors** annually (2025). Language priority based on verified tourist volume data (INE statistics):

| Priority | Language | Tourist Volume | Rationale |
|----------|----------|---------------|-----------|
| 1 | **English** | UK #1 source market (~18M visitors, €24B spend) | Core language. Also covers US, Irish, Australian tourists + digital nomads |
| 2 | **Spanish** | Domestic + LATAM | Essential for domestic tourists + Google.es ranking |
| 3 | **German** | #3 source market (~11M visitors) | Huge volume, high spending, strong "Urlaub" planning culture |
| 4 | **French** | #2 source market (~12M visitors) | Massive volume, many drive to Valencia/Costa Blanca |
| 5 | **Dutch** | Strong market (1M+ in first 5 months 2026) | Over-index on coastal Spain holidays |
| 6 | **Italian** | Significant market | Cultural proximity, growing market |

> [!IMPORTANT]
> English is primary because it captures: UK tourists (largest market), US visitors, digital nomads, and serves as default for any nationality without their own locale. Spanish is second because it captures domestic + LATAM traffic and is essential for local SEO in Google.es.
